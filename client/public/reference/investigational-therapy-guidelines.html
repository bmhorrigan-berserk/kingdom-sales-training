Clinical Positioning Statement

These guidelines are developed and maintained by REM Health Medical Group, P.A. and are utilized in the delivery of adjunctive wellness and performance-based therapies within affiliated clinical programs, including kingdom.

These therapies are considered investigational and are not classified as standard-of-care medical treatments. They are offered selectively based on clinical judgment, patient goals, and individualized risk assessment.

⚠️ IMPORTANT: Investigational Therapy Framework

All peptide therapies in this document are considered investigational and are not FDA-approved for the indications described.

These therapies are not substitutes for standard-of-care medical treatment and should be utilized only in appropriately selected patients.

A lower threshold for therapy modification or discontinuation is appropriate given the investigational nature of these therapies.

General Safety and Prescribing Principles

Patient Selection

Candidates for peptide therapy should: be clinically stable and appropriately screened; have no contraindications based on medical history; demonstrate understanding of the investigational nature of therapy; have realistic expectations regarding outcomes.

Avoid or defer in patients with: active malignancy (unless cleared by an appropriate specialist), uncontrolled chronic disease, pregnancy or breastfeeding, inability to comply with treatment or follow-up.

Informed Consent

All patients must provide informed consent prior to initiation. Consent must include: acknowledgment that therapy is investigational; understanding that FDA approval is not established; discussion of potential risks, benefits, and alternatives; agreement to follow clinical recommendations and monitoring. Documentation of consent must be maintained in the medical record.

Prescribing Considerations

Providers should: individualize therapy based on patient goals and clinical context; avoid protocol-driven prescribing without clinical justification; initiate at conservative dosing; avoid stacking multiple new therapies simultaneously; reassess response prior to dose escalation or addition of new agents.

Follow-Up and Adverse Effects

Follow-up within 6-8 weeks of initiation, then periodically based on therapy and clinical response. Adjust or discontinue therapy in the presence of intolerable side effects, lack of clinical benefit, abnormal laboratory findings, or patient preference. Maintain a low threshold for discontinuation in the absence of clear clinical benefit.

Compounding and Documentation

Peptides should be sourced from reputable compounding pharmacies meeting appropriate regulatory and quality standards. Clinical documentation should include indication for therapy, risk/benefit discussion, informed consent, dosing plan, and follow-up plan.

BPC-157: ADJUNCTIVE PEPTIDE THERAPY

BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound-157) is a synthetic peptide derived from a naturally occurring gastric protein, studied for its potential role in tissue repair, angiogenesis, and modulation of inflammatory pathways. Considered investigational; not FDA-approved for any medical indication.

Indications

  • Musculoskeletal injury (tendon, ligament, or muscle)

  • Chronic joint pain or overuse injury

  • Delayed recovery from training or physical activity

  • Soft tissue inflammation

  • Post-procedural or post-injury recovery support

  • Patients seeking enhanced recovery in a performance-based setting

Dosing and Administration

Standard dose: 500 mcg SC daily. Cycle: 4-8 weeks typical; may extend to 8-12 weeks in chronic or complex cases. Repeat after 4-6 week break based on clinical response. May be injected near the site of injury when feasible. Rotate injection sites; ensure proper sterile technique; reconstitute per pharmacy instructions; refrigerate after mixing.

When Labs Required
No initial labs required
3 Month Peptide Panel CBC, CMP, Hemoglobin A1C, IGF-1
Every 6 Months CBC, CMP, Hemoglobin A1C, IGF-1

Contraindications: active malignancy, pregnancy or breastfeeding, uncontrolled systemic illness

Use caution in patients with history of malignancy or complex unexplained symptoms requiring further evaluation.

Safety Considerations

Providers should monitor for:

  • Injection site irritation

  • Transient headache

  • Dizziness (rare)

⚠️ When Not to Initiate or Continue

• Lack of clinical improvement after an adequate trial (6-8 weeks)

• Worsening symptoms or unclear diagnosis

• Reliance on peptide therapy without addressing underlying pathology

• Patient non-compliance or improper administration

• Should be used as an adjunct, not a replacement for standard orthopedic or rehabilitative care
BPC-157 / TB-500 COMBINATION ("WOLVERINE STACK")

The combination of BPC-157 and TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4 analog) is utilized in performance and recovery-focused settings. TB-500 is associated with cellular migration, angiogenesis, and tissue remodeling; BPC-157 with localized tissue healing and anti-inflammatory effects. This combination is investigational and not FDA-approved for any medical indication.

Indications

  • Moderate to severe musculoskeletal injury

  • Multi-site injury or systemic recovery needs

  • Chronic tendon or ligament pathology

  • Post-surgical recovery support (adjunctive)

  • Suboptimal response to BPC-157 alone

  • High-performance individuals requiring enhanced recovery

Dosing

Component Standard Dose
BPC-157 150 mcg - 500 mcg SC daily
TB-500 250 mcg - 500 mcg SC daily

Cycle: 8-12 weeks typical; reassess at 6-8 weeks for clinical response. Both peptides administered subcutaneously, may be injected near injury site when feasible. Rotate injection sites.

Contraindications: active malignancy, pregnancy or breastfeeding, uncontrolled systemic disease

Use caution in patients with:

  • History of malignancy

  • Complex medical conditions requiring further evaluation

Lab monitoring: not routinely required. Avoid concurrent initiation with multiple other investigational therapies when possible.

Safety Considerations

Providers should monitor for:

  • Injection site irritation

  • Fatigue (occasionally reported)

  • Headache

  • Transient lightheadedness

GLOW PEPTIDE STACK (GHK-Cu / BPC-157 / TB-500)

GHK-Cu (Copper Tripeptide-1) supports tissue remodeling, collagen production, and skin integrity. Combined with BPC-157 (soft tissue healing) and TB-500 (cellular migration and tissue repair), this stack is utilized in select patients for tissue recovery, skin health, and wellness optimization.

Indications

  • Concerns related to skin quality, elasticity, or aging

  • Impaired recovery from physical stress or training

  • Minor soft tissue discomfort or overuse conditions

  • Desire for improved skin appearance or tissue support

  • Adjunctive recovery and wellness optimization

  • Individuals undergoing hormone or metabolic health optimization

Dosing and Administration

Standard starting dose: GHK-Cu 900 mcg / BPC-157 166 mcg / TB-500 333 mcg SC once daily. Cycle: 4-8 weeks per cycle; may extend to 12 weeks based on clinical response. Rotate injection sites; abdomen preferred. Consistent daily dosing at a consistent time each day.

When Labs Required
No initial labs required
3 Month Peptide Panel CBC, CMP, Hemoglobin A1C, IGF-1
Every 6 Months CBC, CMP, Hemoglobin A1C, IGF-1

Contraindications: active malignancy (unless cleared by oncology), pregnancy or breastfeeding, known hypersensitivity to peptide therapies

Use caution in patients with autoimmune conditions, unexplained systemic symptoms, or complex chronic medical conditions.

Safety Considerations

Providers should monitor for:

  • Injection site irritation

  • Skin sensitivity or localized reactions

  • Mild fatigue or headache

  • Transient flushing or irritation

SS-31 (ELAMIPRETIDE): MITOCHONDRIAL SUPPORT

SS-31 (Elamipretide) is a mitochondria-targeted peptide that selectively binds to cardiolipin on the inner mitochondrial membrane, designed to support mitochondrial function, reduce oxidative stress, and improve cellular energy production.

Indications

  • Chronic fatigue or low energy states

  • Impaired recovery from training or physical stress

  • Age-related decline in performance or endurance

  • Mitochondrial dysfunction (suspected or clinically supported)

  • Neurocognitive decline or brain fog

  • Cardiovascular or metabolic optimization goals

  • High oxidative stress burden

  • Advanced recovery and longevity support

Dosing

Starting dose: 5 mg SC once daily. Typical clinical range: 5-10 mg daily. Cycle: 4-8 weeks per cycle; may titrate based on clinical response. Higher dosing (up to 10 mg daily) may be considered in patients with significant fatigue or recovery impairment. Repeat cycles may be performed after a break period.

Administration: SC injection; rotate sites (abdomen preferred); consistent daily dosing recommended.

When Labs Required
No initial labs required
3 Month Peptide Panel CBC, CMP, Hemoglobin A1C, IGF-1
Every 6 Months CBC, CMP, Hemoglobin A1C, IGF-1

Contraindications: active malignancy (unless cleared), pregnancy or breastfeeding, known hypersensitivity

Use caution in patients with:

  • Significant uncontrolled chronic disease

  • Advanced hepatic or renal impairment

  • Complex cardiovascular conditions

Safety Considerations

Providers should monitor for:

  • Injection site irritation

  • Transient fatigue or headache

  • Mild gastrointestinal discomfort

  • Changes in blood pressure or heart rate (rare)

ℹ️ Clinical Notes

• SS-31 is not a hormone and does not directly alter endocrine pathways

• Functions at the mitochondrial level, supporting ATP production and reducing oxidative damage

• May be synergistic with other metabolic or recovery therapies

• Best outcomes often seen when combined with appropriate nutrition, sleep, and training optimization

• Long-term data in wellness populations is limited
RETATRUTIDE: INVESTIGATIONAL TRIPLE AGONIST (GLP-1 / GIP / GLUCAGON)

Retatrutide is an investigational incretin-based therapy acting as a triple agonist at the GLP-1, GIP, and glucagon receptors. Used in select patients to support weight loss, metabolic optimization, and insulin sensitivity. Not classified as standard-of-care.

Mechanism of Action

  • Enhances glucose-dependent insulin secretion

  • Reduces glucagon secretion (GLP-1 effect) while modulating energy expenditure (glucagon receptor)

  • Slows gastric emptying

  • Increases satiety and reduces caloric intake

  • May increase energy expenditure through glucagon-mediated pathways

Indications

  • BMI ≥25

  • BMI <25 requiring maintenance following prior medically supervised weight loss

  • Insulin resistance or prediabetes

  • Type 2 diabetes

  • Metabolic syndrome

  • Difficulty achieving weight loss despite appropriate lifestyle measures

  • Patients requiring advanced metabolic support beyond standard therapies

Dosing: Standard Protocol

Starting dose: 1 mg SC once weekly. Titrate gradually based on tolerance: 1 mg → 2 mg → 4 mg → up to 6-8 mg once weekly. Slower titration may improve tolerability. Higher doses may provide enhanced weight loss but increase side effect risk.

When Labs Required
No initial labs required
3 Month Weight Loss Panel CBC, CMP, TSH, Hemoglobin A1C, Lipid Panel
Every 6 Months CBC, CMP, TSH, Hemoglobin A1C, Lipid Panel

Contraindications: pregnancy, breastfeeding, personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma, MEN2, history of pancreatitis, severe GI disease affecting gastric emptying, known hypersensitivity

Safety Considerations

Providers should monitor for:

  • Nausea and vomiting

  • Constipation

  • Dehydration

  • Gallbladder-related symptoms

  • Excessive appetite suppression or caloric restriction

Dose adjustments should be made based on tolerance and clinical response.

Retatrutide Microdosing Protocol

Standard microdose: 1 mg SC once weekly. Typical range: 1-2 mg weekly based on tolerance. Indications: BMI <25 maintenance, sensitivity to standard incretin dosing, mild appetite dysregulation, metabolic optimization without aggressive weight reduction. Escalation beyond the microdosing range should transition to the standard protocol.

ℹ️ Clinical Notes

• Retatrutide is currently investigational and not FDA-approved

• Demonstrates greater weight loss potential than traditional GLP-1 therapies in early studies

• Triple agonist activity may result in both appetite suppression and increased energy expenditure

• Best outcomes when combined with structured nutrition and exercise programs

• Should be used selectively after risk-benefit assessment

Safety Considerations

Providers should monitor for:

  • Nausea

  • Decreased appetite

  • Constipation

  • Dehydration

  • Excessive caloric restriction

Microdosing is generally associated with improved tolerability compared to standard dosing protocols.

GROWTH HORMONE SECRETAGOGUES

Sermorelin

Sermorelin is a GHRH analog that stimulates the pituitary gland to increase endogenous GH production, leading to downstream increases in IGF-1. Supports physiologic GH production rather than replacing growth hormone directly.

Indications: fatigue, poor sleep quality, reduced recovery, decreased lean muscle mass, increased body fat, age-related decline in GH production

Patient Dose Frequency
Male 500-1000 mcg SC nightly 5-7 nights per week
Female 300 mcg SC nightly 5-7 nights per week

Administer at bedtime, 2-3 hours after the last meal. Nighttime administration aligns with the body's natural GH pulse during sleep.

When Labs Required
No initial labs required
3 Month Peptide Panel CBC, CMP, Hemoglobin A1C, IGF-1
Every 6 Months CBC, CMP, Hemoglobin A1C, IGF-1

⛔ Hard Stop

Patients with active malignancy should not receive GH secretagogue therapy.

Safety Considerations

Providers should monitor for:

  • Injection site irritation

  • Headache

  • Flushing

  • Mild water retention

Patients with active malignancy should not receive growth hormone secretagogue therapy.

Tesamorelin

Synthetic GHRH analog that stimulates pituitary GH secretion and increases IGF-1. Particularly effective for reducing visceral adipose tissue and improving metabolic health.

Indications: visceral fat accumulation, metabolic dysfunction, insulin resistance, reduced exercise recovery, age-related GH decline

Patient Dose Frequency
Male 500-1000 mcg SC nightly 5-6 nights per week
Female 300 mcg SC nightly 5-6 nights per week
When Labs Required
No initial labs required
3 Month Peptide Panel CBC, CMP, Hemoglobin A1C, IGF-1
Every 6 Months CBC, CMP, Hemoglobin A1C, IGF-1

Additional contraindications for tesamorelin: active malignancy, uncontrolled diabetes, pregnancy or breastfeeding

Safety Considerations

Providers should monitor for:

  • Injection site irritation

  • Joint discomfort

  • Water retention

  • Elevated blood glucose

CJC-1295 / Ipamorelin

Ipamorelin is a GHS that stimulates the ghrelin receptor to promote GH release. CJC-1295 is a GHRH analog that enhances pituitary stimulation of GH secretion. Combined, these agents work synergistically to increase physiologic GH pulses and elevate IGF-1 levels.

Indications: poor recovery from exercise, reduced lean muscle mass, increased body fat, fatigue, poor sleep quality

Patient Dose (Ipamorelin / CJC-1295) Frequency
Male 100-200 mcg / 300-400 mcg SC 5 nights per week
Female 50-150 mcg / 150-250 mcg SC 5 nights per week

Administer at bedtime, 2-3 hours after last meal. Some patients may benefit from split dosing (morning and bedtime) depending on treatment goals.

When Labs Required
No initial labs required
3 Month Peptide Panel CBC, CMP, Hemoglobin A1C, IGF-1
Every 6 Months CBC, CMP, Hemoglobin A1C, IGF-1

Safety Considerations

Providers should monitor for:

  • Injection site irritation

  • Flushing

  • Headache

  • Mild water retention

Patients with active malignancy should not receive growth hormone secretagogue therapy.

IGF-1 LR3: ADVANCED ANABOLIC SUPPORT

IGF-1 LR3 (Insulin-Like Growth Factor-1 Long Arginine 3) is a modified analog of IGF-1 designed to promote cellular growth, muscle repair, and recovery. Extended half-life (~20-30 hours) and reduced binding to IGF-binding proteins results in greater biologic activity. Used in carefully selected patients.

Indications

  • Poor recovery from exercise

  • Reduced lean muscle mass

  • Persistent fatigue despite optimized testosterone therapy

  • Plateau in body composition improvement

  • Age-related decline in anabolic signaling

IGF-1 LR3 is typically used after optimization of testosterone and/or GH secretagogue therapy.

⚠️ Use Restriction

IGF-1 LR3 therapy is generally reserved for male patients due to limited safety data in female populations.

Female patients requiring GH support should be considered for GH secretagogue therapy (sermorelin, tesamorelin, or ipamorelin/CJC-1295).

Male Dosing

20-40 mcg SC injection 5 days per week. Standard course: 8-12 weeks on therapy, followed by 4 weeks off. Administer in the morning or post-exercise; rotate injection sites. Post-workout administration may enhance muscle recovery and nutrient uptake (provider discretion).

When Labs Required
No initial labs required
3 Month Peptide Panel CBC, CMP, Hemoglobin A1C, IGF-1
Every 6 Months CBC, CMP, Hemoglobin A1C, IGF-1

Contraindications: active malignancy, proliferative diabetic retinopathy, uncontrolled diabetes mellitus, pregnancy or breastfeeding

Monitor for hypoglycemia symptoms, water retention, headache, joint discomfort. Dose reduction or discontinuation if IGF-1 becomes supraphysiologic or adverse effects occur.

Clinical Safety, Monitoring, and Treatment Oversight

Therapy Modification and Discontinuation

All treatment plans should be evaluated at regular intervals and reassessed at least annually, or more frequently based on clinical need. Therapy may be modified or discontinued based on: development of new contraindications; inadequate clinical response; adverse effects or intolerance; significant laboratory changes; patient preference or non-compliance. Given the investigational nature of these therapies, a lower threshold for modification or discontinuation is appropriate.

Adverse Events and Emergency Response

In the event of a suspected adverse reaction or medical emergency: instruct the patient to seek immediate emergency medical care (call 911) for severe or life-threatening symptoms; promptly assess and discontinue therapy if clinically indicated; document all adverse events thoroughly; complete internal incident reporting per clinic protocol (Exhibit A).

Clinical Responsibility and Documentation

All providers are responsible for: adhering to established clinical protocols; exercising appropriate clinical judgment; maintaining accurate and complete documentation; ensuring informed consent and patient understanding of therapy. All care should reflect a patient-centered, evidence-informed, and safety-focused approach.

Appendix: Dosing Rationale and Clinical Framework

The dosing strategies in this document are derived from early clinical research, translational science, and real-world clinical application within wellness and performance-based medicine. These therapies are considered investigational and are not classified as standard-of-care.

1. Growth Hormone-Modulating Therapies

Dosing informed by: historical clinical use and endocrinology literature, available clinical trials where applicable, physiologic modeling of the GH/IGF-1 axis, and current clinical use patterns in hormone optimization practices.

2. Peptide-Based and Mitochondrial Therapies

Dosing derived from: limited or emerging human data, preclinical and translational research models, compounding pharmacy practices and clinical use patterns, and accumulated real-world clinical experience.

3. General Dosing Principles

  • Dosing is individualized based on clinical presentation, goals, and response

  • Conservative initiation with titration is preferred when appropriate

  • Laboratory and symptom-based monitoring guide adjustments

  • Therapy selection is based on clinician judgment, patient goals, and individualized risk assessment

  • Use of these therapies requires clear patient understanding of their investigational nature
