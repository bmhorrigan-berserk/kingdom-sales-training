Team Operating Guide

Therapy Eligibility, Lab Thresholds, and Escalation Rules

Non-Clinical Staff Reference | Version 1.0 | 2026

⚠️ IMPORTANT: Read Before Using This Guide

This guide is for non-clinical staff to understand eligibility criteria, lab schedules, and when to flag a patient to a provider.

You do NOT make clinical decisions. You route information.

If you are unsure whether something is a patient concern or a lab flag, always escalate to the provider.

This document does not replace provider judgment and does not authorize clinical action by non-clinical team members.

How to Use This Guide

This guide is organized by therapy type. For each therapy, you will find:

Meaning
✅ Green Light What it looks like when a patient qualifies, and everything is normal. No flag needed.
⛔ Red Flag Lab values or situations that must be escalated to a provider immediately.
⚠️ Amber Flag Situations that need provider awareness but are not immediately urgent.
ℹ️ Lab Schedule When labs are due and what to check for.

ℹ️ Your job in the patient journey

1. Confirm the patient has completed required labs before therapy starts.

2. Check that labs are within acceptable range using this guide.

3. Flag anything outside the range to the provider before processing.

4. Follow up on overdue labs and notify the provider.

5. Never tell a patient their labs are "fine" or that they "qualify": that is a provider call.

SECTION 1

Male Hormone Therapies

Male TRT: Testosterone Cypionate Injections

Used for: men with symptoms of low testosterone (fatigue, low libido, ED, muscle loss, mood changes).

✅ A patient is likely eligible if...

• He has completed baseline labs (CBC, CMP, Total Testosterone, Estradiol, Lipid Panel)

• His testosterone is below or at the lower end of the target range AND he has symptoms

• His hematocrit is below 52% and estradiol is below 60 pg/mL

• No active cancer, no recent blood clot (within 12 months), no severe heart disease

• Starting dose: 120 mg weekly SC or IM (or matching prior stable dose if transferring care)

⛔ STOP: Flag to provider immediately if...

• Hematocrit is above 56%: hold on processing

• Estradiol is above 60 pg/mL and patient has symptoms (breast tenderness, water retention)

• PSA was flagged or elevated (age 45+ must have PSA on file)

• Patient reports history of blood clots, cancer, or severe heart disease: provider must clear

• Patient is asking about fertility or trying to conceive: do not proceed to TRT without provider review

⚠️ Flag to provider (not urgent but needed)

• Hematocrit between 52-56%: provider will advise next steps

• RBC above 6.0 or hemoglobin above 18: flag before processing next dose

• Estradiol above 60 pg/mL but patient is asymptomatic: flag, provider decides

ℹ️ Dose Escalation: What You Need to Know

Doses go up in 20 mg increments: 120 → 140 → 160 → 180 → 200 mg.

The provider approves the escalation: you process it. You do not approve it.

Never process a dose above 200 mg/week without explicit provider authorization for that specific patient.

Male TRT: Kyzatrex (Oral Testosterone)

Used for: men with low testosterone symptoms who prefer oral therapy. Must take with fat-containing meals.

✅ Processing is clear if...

• Patient has completed baseline labs (same as injectable TRT)

• He prefers oral over injection or cannot do injections

• Starting dose: 200 mg twice daily with fatty meals (10-12 hours apart)

• Remind patient: MUST take with fat-containing food: skipping fat kills absorption

⛔ STOP: Same red flags as injectable TRT apply here

• Hematocrit, estradiol, PSA thresholds all the same

• Patient reports taking with low-fat meals: flag for provider education note

Oxandrolone: Oral Anabolic Adjunct

Used for: men (and some women) with muscle wasting, poor lean mass response, or recovery needs. Only added AFTER TRT is optimized. NEVER prescribed standalone without TRT for males.

⚠️ This therapy requires extra vigilance on labs

Maximum course: 90 days for men and women. A 30-day break is REQUIRED between courses.

Provider MUST approve a new course. Repeat CMP and lipid panel required before restarting.

This is a 17-alpha-alkylated oral androgen: it stresses the liver and suppresses HDL.

✅ Processing is clear if...

• AST is below 100 IU/L AND ALT is below 100 IU/L AND bilirubin is below 2 mg/dL

• Patient is already on TRT (male patients)

• Provider has explicitly approved the course

• Male dose: 25 mg daily (up to 50 mg only with explicit provider note)

• Female dose: 5 mg daily (10 mg max)

⛔ STOP: Do not process if...

• AST or ALT is above 100 IU/L: flag and hold

• Course has been running more than 90 days without a break

• No 30-day break before restarting a new course

• Patient is female and pregnant or breastfeeding

• Male patient is NOT on TRT without provider confirmation

Nandrolone Decanoate: Joint / Lean Mass Support

Used for: men on TRT with joint pain, tendon issues, or lean mass preservation needs. ONLY for men already on TRT.

✅ Dosing rule

• If patient's weekly testosterone is LESS than 150 mg → nandrolone dose is 50 mg/week

• If patient's weekly testosterone is 150 mg OR MORE → nandrolone dose is 100 mg/week

• Maximum nandrolone: 100 mg/week. Never process above this without explicit provider override.

⛔ STOP: Do not process if...

• Patient is NOT on testosterone therapy

• Patient has history of prostate cancer

• Patient is female: nandrolone is not for female patients in this protocol

• Hematocrit flags apply (see TRT table)

• Significant polycythemia or elevated hematocrit (see TRT hematocrit thresholds)

• Severe dyslipidemia

• Untreated or poorly controlled obstructive sleep apnea
When Labs Required
Every 6 Months CBC, CMP, Total Testosterone, Estradiol, Lipid Panel
Annual CBC, CMP, Total Testosterone, PSA (age 45+ or indicated), Estradiol, Lipid Panel

hCG: Fertility Preservation / Testicular Support

Used for: men on TRT who want to preserve fertility or maintain testicular volume. May also be used standalone for secondary hypogonadism.

✅ Standard hCG protocol

• Typical dose: 250-500 IU SC twice weekly

• Fertility management dose: 1,000 IU three times weekly (provider must specify)

• Patient must have completed baseline labs including LH

⛔ STOP: Flag if...

• Patient's estradiol is above 60 pg/mL before starting: provider must clear

• Patient has active cancer history: provider must clear

• Patient showing signs of gynecomastia (breast tenderness): flag for AI assessment

Annual CBC, CMP, Total/Free Testosterone, Estradiol, Lipid Panel, LH, PSA (age 45+)

Enclomiphene: Testosterone Stimulation

Used for: men who want to increase their own testosterone production without exogenous testosterone. Preferred option for fertility. Dose: 12.5 mg three times weekly (may increase to 25 mg if no response).

⛔ STOP if...

• Patient has had a blood clot within the past 12 months

• Patient has severe liver problems

• Patient has active cancer

⚠️ Flag if patient mentions these symptoms

• Mood changes or irritability

• Visual disturbances or blurred vision: flag immediately

• Persistent headaches
When Labs Required
Baseline CBC, CMP, Total Testosterone, Estradiol, LH
Every 6 Months / Annual Per lab flow chart

AI Management: Anastrozole / Exemestane

Used for: men on TRT with elevated estradiol AND symptoms. NOT for elevated estradiol alone.

ℹ️ The most common team mistake with AI

A patient with a high estradiol number is NOT automatically an AI candidate.

The provider decides. High estradiol without symptoms = no treatment needed.

Do not answer patient questions about "whether their estradiol is too high": route to provider.

✅ First-line AI protocol

• Anastrozole 0.25 mg orally once weekly: first line

• Exemestane 12.5 mg orally once weekly: second line or if anastrozole not tolerated

• Dose can increase to twice weekly if symptoms persist (provider must approve)

⛔ Over-suppression risk: flag these

• Estradiol below 15-20 pg/mL on any lab while on AI: flag immediately

• Patient reports joint pain, fatigue, low libido, or mood changes while on AI

ED Medications: Tadalafil, Sildenafil, PT-141

⛔ HARD STOPS: Do not process if patient reports...

• Using nitrates (nitroglycerin, isosorbide): life-threatening combination with PDE-5 inhibitors

• Uncontrolled high blood pressure (for PT-141)

• Unstable cardiovascular disease: flag to provider for clearance
Medication Standard Dose Key Note
Tadalafil (daily) 2.5-5 mg daily Consistent timing; can take with food
Tadalafil (as needed) 10-20 mg, 30-60 min before Max once per 24 hours
Sildenafil 25-100 mg, 30-60 min before Take on empty stomach for best effect; max once per 24 hours
PT-141 (Bremelanotide) 1-2 mg SC, 30-60 min before Max 8 doses per month; inject abdomen or thigh

SECTION 2

Female Hormone Therapies

Female Testosterone

Used for: women with low libido, fatigue, poor muscle recovery, or menopausal symptoms. Much lower doses than male TRT.

✅ Processing is clear if...

• Patient has completed baseline labs: CBC, CMP, Total Testosterone, Progesterone, Estradiol, Lipid Panel

• Starting dose: 5 mg once weekly SC or IM (or split twice weekly)

• Typical range: 5-10 mg weekly | Target testosterone: 30-80 ng/dL

⛔ STOP: Flag if...

• Patient reports acne, facial hair growth, or voice deepening: provider reviews dosing

• Testosterone above 80 ng/dL and patient is symptomatic
When Labs Required
Baseline CBC, CMP, Total Testosterone, Progesterone, Estradiol, Lipid Panel
6-8 Weeks CBC, CMP, Total Testosterone, Progesterone, Estradiol
Every 6 Months CBC, CMP, Total Testosterone, Progesterone, Estradiol
Annual CBC, CMP, Total Testosterone, Progesterone, Estradiol, Lipid Panel

Estradiol (Transdermal Cream)

Used for: menopausal / perimenopausal symptoms. Applied topically: lower clot risk than oral.

✅ Standard protocol

• Starting dose: 0.5 mg applied topically once daily (inner thigh, wrist, or forearm)

• Typical clinical range: 0.5-1 mg daily | Target estradiol: 40-80 pg/mL for optimal outcomes

• No dose adjustment more frequently than every 6-8 weeks

⚠️ Common situations the team needs to know

• Patient with uterus: she MUST also be on progesterone: flag if progesterone is not in her plan

• Patient complaining of breast tenderness, bloating, or irritability while on estradiol: do not assume she needs more estradiol; flag to provider

• Estradiol above 80 pg/mL and patient still has symptoms: flag to provider before assuming dose needs to increase

⛔ STOP: Do not process if...

• Patient has active cancer (estrogen-sensitive)

• Patient has had a blood clot within the past 12 months

• Patient has unexplained vaginal bleeding: provider must clear before starting

Progesterone

Used for: women with a uterus on estrogen therapy (required for endometrial protection), sleep disturbance, anxiety, mood instability.

✅ Standard protocol

• Dose: 100 mg orally at bedtime (start) | May increase to 200 mg nightly

• Warn patient: causes drowsiness: do not drive after taking

⚠️ Common patient complaints: how to route them

• "I'm too groggy in the morning": flag to provider, may need dose reduction

• "I'm still not sleeping": flag to provider, may need dose increase or timing change

• "I feel more anxious": flag to provider, can happen at wrong dose levels

ℹ️ The estrogen / progesterone balance rule (simplified)

If a female patient is on estradiol AND has symptoms like breast tenderness, bloating, or mood swings:

1. DO NOT assume she needs more estradiol.

2. The provider may need to increase progesterone first.

3. Route any symptom report involving these two hormones to the provider.

Weight Loss Therapies

GLP-1 Therapies: Semaglutide and Tirzepatide

✅ Eligibility

• BMI 25 or above

• BMI below 25 ONLY if they previously completed medically supervised weight loss and need maintenance

• No labs required before starting: labs required at the 3-month mark

⛔ HARD STOPS: Do not process if patient reports...

• Personal or family history of medullary thyroid cancer or MEN2

• History of pancreatitis

• Pregnancy or breastfeeding

• Severe GI disease or gastroparesis

Phentermine

Short-term appetite suppression. BMI 30+ or BMI 27+ with weight-related health conditions. Dose: 37.5 mg in the morning (start 18.75 mg for sensitive patients). Duration: typically 8-12 weeks.

⛔ HARD STOPS

• Uncontrolled high blood pressure

• Heart disease or arrhythmias

• Hyperthyroidism

• Glaucoma

• History of drug or stimulant abuse

• Pregnancy or breastfeeding

• Taking MAOIs

Lipo B and L-Carnitine

Energy and fat metabolism support. No required labs. Lipo B: 1 mL weekly SC or IM. L-Carnitine: 500 mg (1 mL) IM twice weekly.

⛔ Flag if...

• Patient has severe liver disease (Lipo B: relative contraindication)

• Patient reports history of seizures (L-Carnitine: flag to provider)

SECTION 5

Investigational Peptide Therapies

⚠️ IMPORTANT: Peptide Therapy Context

All peptide therapies are investigational: not FDA-approved for the indications used.

Informed consent is required before any peptide therapy can be processed.

If informed consent is not documented, hold the order and flag to the provider.

When patients ask if peptides are "proven" or "FDA-approved," the correct response is:

"These are investigational therapies used under medical supervision. Your provider can answer specific questions about them."

Universal Escalation and Lab Thresholds

Universal Escalation Rules: Apply Across ALL Therapies

Always Escalate Immediately Always Route to Provider Before Proceeding

• Patient reports chest pain, shortness of breath, or leg swelling

• Hematocrit above 56% on any male TRT patient

• AST or ALT above 100 IU/L on any oral androgen patient

• Patient reports visual disturbances on enclomiphene

• Any suspected allergic reaction to an injectable

• Patient mentions blood clot, stroke, or ER visit

• Informed consent is not on file for any peptide therapy

• A patient's lab is outside any threshold in this guide

• Patient mentions pregnancy or trying to conceive

• Patient adds a new prescription medication

• Any cancer history not previously documented

• Patient has been off therapy more than 90 days
